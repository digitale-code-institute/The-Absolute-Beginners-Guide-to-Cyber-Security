Welcome to the section review. Below are the key takeaways from this section

  • IMAP and POP are the two primary protocols used for receiving emails
  • SMTP and STARTTLS are the two primary protocols used for sending emails
  • Phishing attacks involve the use of emails crafted to entice the victim to click on the links or attachments provided in the email
  • Contrary to popular belief, opening and reading a phishing email with malicious links is not harmful. The real harm occurs when the user either clicks on the link inside of the email or downloads the attachments.
